Angular Size of Solar System Objects

  The largest solar sysem objects (and Betelegsuese) to scale based on their angular diameters. Click image for Original Size.

  The interior planets are shown in crescents to repersent their maximum angular diameters at their inferior conjunctions, and disks to repersent their minimum diameters at their superior conjunctions. The variation in size of each planet and moon system repersent their closest and furthest approaches to earth, and thus their maximum and minum angular diameters. The only exception is the star Betelgeuse, whose change is largely due to the star's instability. Additionally, Uranus and more distant planets are shown with the distances between moons to scale.

  The Images were obtained from a wide variety of sources, including the NASA Mariner 10, Messenger, Galileo , Voyager, New Horizions and Cassini spacecraft and the Hubble Space Telescope.
